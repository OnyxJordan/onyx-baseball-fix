Model Notes

Applied to today's projections

Career Regression
Bayesian regression toward position averages. Players with fewer than 300 MLB PA are pulled toward baseline, so hot-start rookies cannot distort the model.
Park x Wind Interaction
Every park carries its own wind sensitivity. Wrigley's open bowl amplifies a blowing-out reading; Oracle, Comerica, Petco, and Kauffman are locked neutral because their gauge readings rarely translate to real carry.
Air Density
Temperature, humidity, and barometric pressure all feed the environment multiplier. Humid air is lighter than dry air, and low pressure means thinner air: both add carry.
Platoon + Pitcher Blend
Batter hand vs pitcher hand adjustment on every matchup, with the pitcher factor blended from xFIP, HR/9, HR/FB, ground ball rate, and barrel rate allowed.
Recency + Due Meter
Last-14-day form from live MLB data shapes each projection, and the due meter flags power hitters in an HR drought whose quality metrics say the surge is coming.
Bullpen Exposure
Roughly 40% of plate appearances come against relievers, so each team's bullpen HR/9 is blended into the edge lane alongside the starter.
Each Day

What gets updated in every build

Fully Automated Data
Lineups and probables from the MLB Stats API with roster fallback, fresh weather from Open-Meteo for every park, and last-14-day hitter and pitcher form from live MLB aggregates. No uploads, no spreadsheets.
Odds + Game Lines
HR props pulled per game with DraftKings book preference, plus totals and moneylines merged into every game card. Stale or missing odds are gated honestly: no edges shown, no picks logged, never fake numbers.
Top 5 Tracker
The top 5 plays from the board are logged automatically each day and graded from final boxscores the next morning. The SB Record tab is exactly that list, wins, losses, and P&L, with zero manual entry.
Hourly Refresh
From 1:30 to 7:30 PM ET the pipeline reruns every hour, picking up confirmed lineups, weather shifts, and line moves. The site redeploys only when something actually changed.
Fail-Safe Builds
A build that scores zero players aborts and leaves the previous page live. Off days exit clean. A broken data source can never ship a blank board.
Live Layer
The score ticker and Gamecenter poll the MLB Stats API from your browser. When a modeled batter homers, play-by-play detection lights him up across the ticker, the board, and the Gamecenter within seconds.
The Engine

How Onyx works — inside the model

The Origin

Where Onyx came from

Onyx Baseball started with a simple frustration: HR prop tools either regurgitate odds without adding insight, or drown you in raw statcast numbers with no clear signal. Neither tells you where the market is actually wrong.

The goal was to build something different — a model that starts from a player's genuine power profile (not just last week's hot streak), adjusts it for who they're facing that day, where they're playing, what the weather is doing, and whether the market has priced all of that in. Then surfaces only the plays where there's a real gap between our estimate and the line.

It runs every morning on real lineup data, live odds, Statcast metrics, and weather feeds. What you're looking at is the output.

The Model

What goes into every probability

Each player's HR probability is built from the ground up — not borrowed from the sportsbook. Here's every component that feeds it:

Career HR Rate
Bayesian-regressed career HR/PA anchored to position average. Short MLB samples (under 300 PA) are pulled toward the positional mean — preventing hot-start rookies from being over-rated.
Foundation
Home / Away Split
Career HR/PA splits by home and away context, applied to where they're playing today. Split only trusted when career sample exceeds 200 PA, capped at ±15% of career rate to prevent noise.
Foundation
Statcast Quality (SC Score)
Blended signal from barrel rate, exit velocity (EV90), hard-hit%, and ISO. Weighted 70% current season / 30% 3-year career when data is sufficient. Capped 0.60–1.20 to prevent outliers from distorting raw rates.
Quality Signal
L14 Recency Form
Last 14 days of HR/PA compared to the regressed career base. Adjusts the base rate up or down, capped at 0.80–1.15 to prevent small hot or cold streaks from swinging the model too far.
Recency
Pitcher Matchup Factor
Blended from the starter's xFIP and L14 HR/9, weighted against their career profile. Scales 0.60–1.50, with heavier L14 weight (up to 30%) only when the pitcher has 5+ recent innings. Accounts for handedness via L/R split.
Matchup
Park HR Factor
Historical HR park index for each stadium. Oracle Park and Petco (0.88) suppress HRs meaningfully; GABP and Wrigley (1.09–1.12) inflate them. Also feeds directly into the composite score — suppressor park edge plays are discounted because the market prices these well.
Environment
Wind & Weather
Wind speed and direction translated to an HR environment factor: out-blowing wind at Wrigley can add 5–8%; stiff crosswind or in-blowing wind subtracts. Temperature affects ball flight — cold air is denser. Roof status locks env_factor at 1.0.
Environment
Due Meter
Tracks how a player's actual HR pace compares to their expected pace over their last stretch of PA. OVERDUE (+14%), DUE (+7%), COOL (+2%), WARM (−3%), HOT (−7%), FIRE (−12%). Reflects regression-to-mean pressure, not streakiness.
Timing
Market Calibration
Model probability is blended with DraftKings implied probability. Shorter odds (sharper markets) get more market weight: ≤+300 is 60/40 model/market; longer odds (+650+) are 75/25. This corrects for model overconfidence in well-priced favorites.
Market
Composite Score
Final ranking metric combining probability (35%), edge over market (50%), and expected value (15%). Gated by minimum probability thresholds. Then multiplied by a park quality factor — Oracle/Petco plays are discounted 20% because market efficiency is higher there.
Output
Build History

How the model has evolved

Onyx launched on May 6, 2026. Everything in the calibration log below happened within 18 days. The model is still early — the record reflects that — but the structural improvements are real and the projection foundation is now solid.

May 23 — current
Projection model v13: slash-line DFS + base rate regression
DFS projections rebuilt from scratch. Previous system was HR-only (hr_prob × 0.12 × multiplier) — singles, doubles, walks, runs, RBI, and stolen bases were completely ignored. v13 replaced it with a full slash-line model: xwOBA drives contact rates, hr_prob drives HR, ISO drives doubles, EV90 drives walks, HH% proxies stolen bases, with empirical R/RBI tables by batting order. Global SCALE=0.86 calibrated against a reference projection system on 69 matched players (FD MAE 1.00pt, DK MAE 0.82pt). Also fixed: sc_score was being applied twice — once inside hr_prob, again in the hit rate formula — causing ~16% systematic overestimation across all contact projections. Separately, career HR/PA rates now Bayesian-regressed toward position averages (k=250 PA pseudocount) to stop small-sample 2026 call-ups from inflating the model.
May 22
Calibration pass: SC, composite, due meter, weather, UI
Six changes shipped the same day after a structural audit. SC score cap tightened (0.20–1.80 → 0.60–1.35). Pitcher factor cap lowered (1.80 → 1.50). L14 form adjustment tightened (±25% → ±20%). Due meter converted from additive flat rate to multiplicative 7-tier scale (OVERDUE ×1.30 / FIRE ×0.88). Composite formula rebuilt edge-first: 0.35×prob + 0.50×edge + 0.15×EV, gated by minimum probability thresholds, then multiplied by the due tier. Game diversity discount added to prevent over-concentration on one game (rank 1: ×1.00 down to rank 5+: ×0.55). Weather engine upgraded to park-specific IN/OUT/Cross wind classification using each stadium's HR corridor orientation. No-odds penalty introduced (composite×0.003 buries players without posted props). Plays tab rebuilt with top-5 limit, confidence 30–98 scale, three reasoning lines per play, and barrel% stat box. DFS Record tab added with cumulative P&L chart.
May 21
CAREER_DB + PITCHER_CAREER_DB baked in, logo processing
687 hitter career profiles and 828 pitcher profiles embedded directly in the shell HTML — eliminates rebuilding from scratch each day. CAREER_DB schema: career HR/PA overall + home/away splits, 3-year and 2026 season EV90, barrel%, HH%, ISO, and PA counts. Logo processing rebuilt with adaptive flood fill and navy pill background (#1c3252) for consistent team badge rendering across all 30 teams.
May 6–20
Initial build and early iteration
Launched with a base model: career HR/PA + home/away splits + L14 recency form adjustment + pitcher xFIP factor + DraftKings odds calibration. Market blending tiered by odds length. Statcast SC score introduced using barrel rate, EV90, hard-hit%, and ISO. Park HR indices and wind/temperature environment factor added. The composite score was established as the primary ranking signal — edge vs market (50%), probability (35%), expected value (15%). The record from this period (11-43 overall through May 23) reflects both the model finding its footing and the genuine difficulty of the problem: HR props are priced efficiently. The signal is real; the edges are small.
On the Roadmap

What we're building next

These are the known structural gaps in the current model — things that would improve accuracy if solved, roughly in order of expected impact.

Platt Scaling
Fit a logistic calibration curve mapping raw model output to true empirical probabilities — sharpens the edge calculation. Needs 50+ resolved outcomes. Getting close.
Soon
Hit Rate by Probability Bucket
Track actual HR hit rate vs model probability in ranges (0–10%, 10–18%, 18–25%, 25%+). Identifies where the model is systematically over or under-confident. Feeds back into calibration.
In progress
Speed Data for SB Model
Current SB estimate: flat 0.087/game × hard-hit speed proxy. No sprint speed in CAREER_DB yet. Next: manual speed tier table for known burners, or pull Statcast Sprint Speed from daily CSV.
Planned
Pull% × Park Interaction
A dead-pull hitter at GABP is meaningfully different from a spray hitter. Directional barrel data + park-specific HR distances would replace the blunt park factor with a player-park interaction term.
Planned
DFS Lineup Performance Tracking
Currently logging entry/win totals only. Need per-lineup scoring to identify which composite signals translate to DFS finishes vs which are noise. Informs how to weight model factors in lineup construction.
Planned
CAREER_DB Refresh (~June 4)
Current DB was built on data through late May. Refresh planned for early June — more 2026 PA for all players, updated career rates, Statcast metrics, and split data with a fuller season sample. Also expands R/RBI BO tables from 69 to full-slate training set.
